Teaching Philosophy

I believe in creating an interactive learning environment where students are encouraged to ask questions, experiment with concepts, and apply their knowledge to real-world problems.

My approach to teaching and mentorship is centered around three core principles:

Hands-on Learning

I emphasize practical application of concepts through project-based learning, allowing students to develop intuition and problem-solving skills through direct experience.

Critical Thinking

I encourage students to question assumptions, evaluate methodologies critically, and develop their own perspectives on technical challenges.

Inclusive Collaboration

I foster a collaborative environment where diverse viewpoints are valued, and students learn from each other as well as from the instructor.
