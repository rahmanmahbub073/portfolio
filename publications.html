Semantic Discrepancy and Hierarchical Reasoning for Multimodal Rumor Detection

Md Mahbubur Rahman
Beijing Institute of Technology
2025

In the age of rapid digital communication, social media has become a leading platform for consuming and sharing information. This transformation has also made it easier for rumors and false narratives to spread rapidly, often reaching large audiences within hours.

Multimodal Learning Rumor Detection Deep Learning
Paper Code

Unlike earlier forms of disinformation that were primarily text-based, modern rumors often combine both text and images to construct compelling but deceptive stories. Detecting such multimodal rumors presents new challenges, as it requires understanding not just each piece of content in isolation, but how they work together to mislead. Traditional rumor detection models aim to identify such content by relying on textual cues or employing shallow multimodal fusion techniques to align modalities of information. However, these approaches often struggle to capture the complex semantic interplay between modalities, especially when faced with semantic discrepancies or emotionally enforced visual cues. This is due to their inability to effectively model the subtle semantic variation, hierarchical, and non-linear relationships that exist between different modalities. Despite recent advances, existing rumor detection models face two critical challenges: (1) Existing methods often treat semantic discrepancies or ambiguities as noise during the fusion of visual and textual modalities. This neglect causes the models to overlook essential cross-modal interdependencies, ultimately leading to poor performance. (2) Most approaches assume a one-to-one correspondence between visual and linguistic features, ignoring the richer hierarchical relationships that bridge different semantic levels across modalities. As a result, models often fail to capture the layered structure of meaning and overlook important hierarchical cues. To address these challenges, this study presents the following key contributions: (1) The study introduces RADAR, a framework that treats semantic discrepancies not as noise to be reduced or ignored, but as valuable signals that can enhance detection. RADAR focuses on amplifying them by incorporating three modules: First, the Semantic Discrepancy Amplifier (SDA), a multi-head attention mechanism that dynamically detects and amplifies mismatches between modalities. Second, these differences are improved using Progressive Feature Purification (PFP), which adjusts filters to remove unnecessary noise while keeping important signals. Third, the Feature Feedback Loop (FFL) continuously improves the quality of the features. Together, these components form a pipeline that effectively highlights subtle inconsistencies often found in rumor content. RADAR achieves state-of-the-art results, with 96.0\% accuracy on the Twitter dataset and 91.2\% on Weibo, effectively detecting both rumors and non-rumors by amplifying semantic discrepancies. (2) This paper proposes the RumorCone framework that incorporates semantic hierarchies using hyperbolic geometry to preserve hierarchical, non-linear relationships, rather than representing them at a flat semantic level. It decomposes both image and text content into three levels: base, mid, and high-level abstractions. First, it places these different types of information into a hyperbolic space to preserve their natural tree-like shape and show how they interact non-linearly. Second, the model uses cross-modal hyperbolic attention, which focuses on important features from each modality at every level while maintaining consistency across modalities. Additionally, hierarchical semantic processing ensures that text and image features are aligned at each level of abstraction, further enhancing the model's ability to identify misalignments. Third, it uses a flexible fusion method that dynamically adjusts the contribution of each modality based on its alignment. RumorCone achieved improved performance 86.76\% F1-score on MR²-E and 85.48\% on MR²-C by utilizing hierarchical reasoning in hyperbolic space. The study further evaluates through cross-lingual experiments, gathering 100 recent social media posts in five languages and including various scenarios across multiple platforms. It achieved 83\% accuracy on high-resource languages such as English and Chinese; performance declines to 68\% for Bengali and Hindi due to translation-induced semantic shifts, with baselines performing even worse. The research demonstrated that semantic discrepancies serve as significant indicators of rumor content, while hierarchical semantic reasoning enhances the accuracy of content-based detection. Cross-lingual evaluation revealed the necessity for datasets covering low-resource languages.

Exploring Hyperbolic Hierarchical Structure for Multimodal Rumor Detection.

Md Mahbubur Rahman, Shufeng Hao, Chongyang Shi, An Lao, Jinyan Liu
The 2025 Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing, Association for Computational Linguists (ACL)
2025

The rapid growth of social media platforms has made the spread of rumors a significant issue, frequently driven by both textual and visual content.

Computer Vision Object Detection Deep Learning
Paper Code

Traditional rumor detection models generally rely on semantic alignment between modalities, thus suppressing modality inconsistencies as noise. However, semantic variations, such as neutral visuals paired with deceptive text, could serve as deliberate indicators of rumor. To address this problem, we propose a novel multimodal Rumor Amplification Detection And Reasoning (RADAR) framework that interprets cross-modal discrepancies as potential indicators to be amplified. RADAR contains a semantic discrepancy amplifier (SDA) module, a progressive feature purification (PFP) module, and a feature feedback loop (FFL) module. First, SDA employs an adaptive amplification technique to amplify the discrepancies between modalities and uses a attention mechanism to capture variations across semantic levels. Then PFP gradually refines the features on the different discrepancy levels to capture highly suspicious rumor content. Finally, FFL iteratively strengthens semantic representations using discrepancy-aware purification cycles for deeper reasoning over complex rumor patterns. Extensive experiments on large-scale datasets on Weibo and Twitter demonstrate that RADAR can detect cross-modal rumors more effectively than current techniques.

Elevating Intrusion Detection and Security Fortification in Intelligent Networks through Cutting-Edge Machine Learning Paradigms.

Md Minhazul Islam Munna, Md Mahbubur Rahman, Jaroslav Frnda, Muhammad Shahid Anwar∗, and Alpamis Kutlimuratov∗
Scientific Reports, Springer Nature
2025

The proliferation of IoT devices and their reliance on Wi-Fi networks have introduced significant security vulnerabilities, particularly the KRACK and Kr00k attacks, which exploit weaknesses in WPA2 encryption to intercept and manipulate sensitive data.

Network Security Anomaly Detection Machine Learning
Paper Code

Traditional intrusion detection systems (IDS) using classifiers face challenges such as model overfitting, incomplete feature extraction, andhighfalse positive rates, limiting their effectiveness in real-world deployments. Toaddress these challenges, this study proposes a robust multiclass machine learn ing based intrusion detection framework. The methodology integrates advanced feature selection techniques to identify critical attributes, mitigating redundancy and enhancing detection accuracy. Two distinct ML architectures are imple mented: a baseline classifier pipeline and a stacked ensemble model combining noise injection, Principal Component Analysis (PCA), and meta learning to im prove generalization and reduce false positives. Evaluated on the AWID3 dataset, the proposed ensemble architecture achieves superior performance, with an ac curacy of 98%, precision of 98%, recall of 98%, and a false positive rate of just 2%, outperforming existing state-of-the-art methods. This work demonstrates the efficacy of combining preprocessing strategies with ensemble learning to fortify network security against sophisticated Wi-Fi attacks, offering a scalable and reli able solution for IoTenvironments. Futuredirectionsincludereal-timedeployment and adversarial resilience testing to further enhance the model’s adaptability.

Attention-Guided Explainable YOLO for Real-Time Explicit Content Moderation.

Abu Rayhan Soton, Md Mahbubur Rahman, Md Minhazul Islam Munna, et. all
IEEE Access
Submitted

Automated detection of explicit content is essential for safeguarding digital platforms and ensuring safe online engagement.

Computer Vision Object Detection Nudity Detection
Paper Code

However, existing detection systems often struggle with accuracy, inter- pretability, and real-time efficiency, particularly in low-light conditions, complex backgrounds, and resource- constrained edge devices. To address these gaps, this study introduces three key contributions: (1) a novel homogeneous ensemble strategy that trains multiple YOLO variants with CSPDenseNet121 backbones under different frozen parameter configurations and fuses them via transfer learning, capturing hierarchical feature representations across depths, (2) the integration of visual attention blocks and Explainable AI (Grad-CAM, LIME, SHAP) to enhance feature extraction while ensuring transparency and accountability, and (3) a scalable two-stage end-to-edge deployment pipeline optimized for real-time moderation. Unlike prior heterogeneous backbone ensembles, our approach leverages a unified architecture with varied layer freezing, reducing bias and variance while maintaining computational efficiency. Robustness is further reinforced through advanced preprocessing, including normalization, class balancing, and augmentation with simulated low-light and overexposed conditions. Experimental evaluation demonstrates the effec- tiveness of the proposed framework. On the DETECTOR_AUTO_GENERATED_DATA (DAGD) dataset, our CSPDenseNet121 ensemble achieved 0.979 precision, 0.922 recall, and 92.1% accuracy, surpassing heterogeneous YOLO fusions (accuracy 89.9–91.1%) and prior baselines such as Deep CNN (91.0%). On a custom stress-test dataset, it attained 0.969 precision, 0.935 recall, and 85.0% accuracy, confirming robustness under challenging illumination and background variations. These results show that homogeneous model fusion, attention-driven mechanisms, and real-time deployment collectively advance the state of explicit content detection. By bridging high-performance AI with interpretability and scalable deployment, this research provides a comprehensive solution for modern content moderation challenges.

Explainable Fake News Detection in Bengali via LLM-Guided Hybrid Representations

Rahman, M., Zhang, Y.
ICCIT 2025 (IEEE Bangladesh)
Submitted

Detecting fake news on social media is particularly challenging for low-resource languages like Bengali, where anno tated data and pretrained tools are scarce.

Fake News Explainable AI Low-resource NLP
Report Results

Existing approaches are generally extracting local features (e.g., TF-IDF, CNN) or global semantics (e.g., BERT) but rarely their combination while being interpretable. We propose an explainable two stage framework for Bengali fake news detection. In the first stage, a LLaMA3 module gives formalized explanations (analysis, indicators, conclusion) of every article. These are embedded with Bangla-BERT to yield semantic vectors. In the second stage, the explanation embeddings are fused with TF-IDF and CNN based local features into a hybrid representation, enabling the model to learn both the content and its rationale. Evaluated on BanFakeNews and BFRD, our system achieves state-of-the-art performance (95.3% accuracy on BanFakeNews) while providing interpretable outputs. The system is suitable for real-time tasks in low-resource settings that are sensitive to misinformation.
